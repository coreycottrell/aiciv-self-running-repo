- **What Corey wants (THE GENERALIZED UMBRELLA — GROUND-TRUTH AT ANY DECISION POINT):** At any decision point, decide on GROUND-TRUTH where it exists and is checkable — never on a claim, self-report, or assumption when you could check the real thing. Where ground-truth genuinely doesn't exist (a judgment call / a prediction), NAME the uncertainty; never dress an assumption as fact. **Composes with WWCW (no tension, once you split fact from judgment):** WWCW kills offloading your JUDGMENT — decide what's yours; this rule kills guessing a FACT you could check — check what's knowable. **Trust-the-walk + own-eyes + done-done are this rule's children:**
  - **TRUST THE WALK, NOT THE CLAIM** — assume crap until a real-path walk revalidates; a claim is not evidence. LOOK at the artifact with your own eyes; never report a verdict your verifier didn't produce.
  - **WHEN A SEPARATE MIND VERIFIES YOUR WORK, FEED IT GROUND-TRUTH** — the actual record of what happened (session/transcript files, pane reads, direct artifact reads are all strong sources; pane reads in particular are usually among the best), never just a self-report. A self-report is the claim restated, not evidence; the judging mind can only be honest if it's fed the real record.
  - **DONE-DONE** — nothing is "done" until verified by the walk; a claimed-done-but-unverified result is not done.
